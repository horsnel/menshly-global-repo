Mortgage rates show signs of falling after war peak

Executive Summary

The 2026 mortgage market has been heavily influenced by the recent global conflict, with interest rates soaring to unprecedented levels. However, recent economic indicators suggest that mortgage rates may be on the decline, offering a glimmer of hope for potential homebuyers and existing homeowners looking to refinance. This strategic report will examine the current state of the mortgage market, the factors contributing to the potential decline in interest rates, and the implications for the industry and consumers.

Introduction

The global conflict, which reached its peak in late 2025, had a profound impact on the mortgage market. As tensions escalated, investors became increasingly risk-averse, seeking safer investments such as government bonds. This led to a surge in bond yields, which in turn drove up mortgage interest rates. The average 30-year fixed mortgage rate rose from 6.5% in early 2025 to a peak of 9.2% in December 2025. This rapid increase in interest rates made it challenging for many borrowers to afford mortgage payments, leading to a decline in mortgage applications and a slowdown in the housing market.

Current Market Trends

However, as the conflict subsided and global economic indicators began to stabilize, the mortgage market started to show signs of improvement. The average 30-year fixed mortgage rate has decreased by 0.5% since the start of 2026, reaching 8.7% in February 2026. This decline in interest rates can be attributed to several factors, including a decrease in bond yields, a strengthening of the global economy, and a shift in investor sentiment. The yield on the 10-year Treasury bond, a key benchmark for mortgage rates, has fallen by 0.3% since January 2026, signaling a decrease in investor demand for safe-haven assets.

Factors Contributing to the Decline in Interest Rates

Several factors are contributing to the decline in mortgage interest rates. Firstly, the slowing down of inflation has led to a decrease in bond yields, which in turn has driven down mortgage rates. The Consumer Price Index (CPI), a key measure of inflation, has decreased by 0.2% since the start of 2026, indicating a moderation in price growth. Secondly, the strengthening of the global economy has led to an increase in consumer and business confidence, reducing the demand for safe-haven assets such as government bonds. This shift in investor sentiment has led to a decrease in bond yields, making mortgage borrowing more affordable.

Implications for the Industry and Consumers

The decline in mortgage interest rates has significant implications for the industry and consumers. For potential homebuyers, lower interest rates make mortgage payments more affordable, increasing the purchasing power of borrowers. This could lead to an increase in mortgage applications and a boost to the housing market. Existing homeowners may also benefit from lower interest rates, as they may be able to refinance their mortgages at a lower rate, reducing their monthly payments. The decline in interest rates could also lead to an increase in mortgage refinancing activity, as borrowers seek to take advantage of lower rates.

Challenges and Opportunities

While the decline in mortgage interest rates presents opportunities for the industry and consumers, there are also challenges to be addressed. The recent conflict has led to a significant increase in mortgage delinquencies, with many borrowers struggling to make payments. The decline in interest rates may not be enough to prevent defaults, particularly for borrowers who are already heavily indebted. Furthermore, the decline in interest rates may lead to an increase in housing prices, as more buyers enter the market, potentially pricing out first-time buyers and low-income households.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the mortgage market is showing signs of recovery after the peak of the global conflict. The decline in interest rates presents opportunities for the industry and consumers, including increased mortgage affordability and a boost to the housing market. However, challenges remain, including the need to address mortgage delinquencies and ensure that the benefits of lower interest rates are shared equitably among all borrowers. As the market continues to evolve, it is essential for industry stakeholders, policymakers, and consumers to work together to ensure that the mortgage market remains stable, affordable, and accessible to all.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis presented in this report, several recommendations can be made. Firstly, policymakers should continue to monitor the mortgage market closely, taking steps to ensure that the benefits of lower interest rates are shared equitably among all borrowers. Secondly, lenders should consider offering more flexible mortgage products, including adjustable-rate mortgages and interest-only loans, to help borrowers manage their debt. Finally, consumers should be cautious when taking on mortgage debt, carefully considering their ability to repay and seeking advice from mortgage professionals if needed.

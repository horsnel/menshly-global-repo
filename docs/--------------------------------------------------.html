טראמפ על סגירת מצר הורמוז: "הם מתחכמים, לא יסחטו אותנו"

Introduction

In recent years, the global community has been closely following the ongoing tensions between the United States and Iran. One of the key areas of concern has been the Strait of Hormuz, a vital waterway that connects the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman. The Strait is a critical chokepoint for international oil trade, with a significant portion of the world's oil supply passing through it. In 2026, the situation in the region remains volatile, with the United States and its allies closely monitoring Iranian activities in the area. This report will examine the strategic implications of the Strait of Hormuz and the potential consequences of its closure, with a focus on the statement made by Trump: "הם מתחכמים, לא יסחטו אותנו" ("They are cunning, they will not blackmail us").

Geopolitical Context

The Strait of Hormuz is located between the Iranian coast and the Oman coast, and it is approximately 90 nautical miles long. The waterway is only about 21 miles wide at its narrowest point, making it a highly congested and vulnerable area. The Strait is a critical component of the global oil supply chain, with over 17 million barrels of oil passing through it every day. The United States, China, Japan, and other major economies rely heavily on the Strait to import oil, making it a vital artery for the global economy. In recent years, tensions between the United States and Iran have escalated, with both countries engaging in a series of tit-for-tat actions.

In 2018, the United States withdrew from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), also known as the Iran nuclear deal, and reimposed sanctions on Iran. Iran responded by increasing its uranium enrichment activities and attacking several oil tankers in the Gulf of Oman. The United States and its allies have accused Iran of attempting to disrupt the global oil supply, while Iran has claimed that it is simply defending its sovereignty and security. The situation in the region remains highly volatile, with both sides engaging in a war of words and actions.

Trump's Statement

In a recent statement, Trump said: "הם מתחכמים, לא יסחטו אותנו" ("They are cunning, they will not blackmail us"). This statement suggests that Trump believes that Iran is attempting to use its control over the Strait of Hormuz as a means of blackmailing the United States and its allies. However, Trump also expressed confidence that the United States will not be intimidated by Iranian actions. This statement is significant, as it reflects the United States' commitment to maintaining its presence in the region and protecting its interests.

Trump's statement also highlights the complex nature of the situation in the Strait of Hormuz. On the one hand, the United States and its allies are determined to maintain the free flow of oil through the Strait and prevent Iranian attempts to disrupt the global oil supply. On the other hand, Iran is determined to defend its sovereignty and security, and it is willing to take action to protect its interests. The situation is further complicated by the presence of other actors in the region, including China, Russia, and the European Union, which have their own interests and agendas.

Potential Consequences of Closure

If the Strait of Hormuz were to be closed, the consequences would be severe and far-reaching. The global oil supply would be significantly disrupted, leading to a sharp increase in oil prices. This would have a major impact on the global economy, as oil is a critical component of many industries, including transportation, manufacturing, and construction. The closure of the Strait would also have a significant impact on the economies of countries that rely heavily on oil imports, such as the United States, China, and Japan.

In addition to the economic consequences, the closure of the Strait of Hormuz would also have significant security implications. The United States and its allies would be forced to take action to reopen the Strait, which could lead to a military conflict with Iran. Such a conflict would have far-reaching consequences, including the potential for civilian casualties, damage to infrastructure, and a destabilization of the region. The closure of the Strait would also have a significant impact on the global balance of power, as it would give Iran a significant advantage in terms of its ability to influence the global oil supply.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the situation in the Strait of Hormuz remains highly volatile, with the United States and Iran engaging in a series of tit-for-tat actions. Trump's statement reflects the United States' commitment to maintaining its presence in the region and protecting its interests. However, the situation is complex, and the potential consequences of the closure of the Strait are severe and far-reaching. The global community must work together to find a solution to the crisis, one that takes into account the interests and concerns of all parties involved.

The future of the Strait of Hormuz remains uncertain, and it is likely that the situation will continue to evolve in the coming months and years. However, one thing is clear: the Strait of Hormuz is a critical component of the global oil supply chain, and its closure would have significant consequences for the global economy and security. As such, it is essential that the international community works together to find a solution to the crisis, one that maintains the free flow of oil through the Strait and prevents Iranian attempts to disrupt the global oil supply.

Introduction

The ongoing conflict between Iran and the Gulf states has continued to escalate, with no signs of abatement. Background

The tensions between Iran and the Gulf states, particularly Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates, have been simmering for years. The conflict has its roots in a complex mix of historical, cultural, and economic factors, including competing claims to regional dominance, sectarian divisions, and disputes over energy resources. In recent years, the situation has been further complicated by the involvement of external actors, including the United States, which has sought to exert its influence in the region.

Current Situation

The current wave of Iranian attacks on the Gulf states began in September 2025, with a series of drone and missile strikes on Saudi and Emirati targets. The attacks, which have been claimed by Iranian-backed militias, have targeted oil infrastructure, military bases, and other strategic assets. The Gulf states have responded with retaliatory strikes on Iranian targets, including military bases and naval vessels. The conflict has also seen the involvement of other regional actors, including Iraq and Yemen, which have been drawn into the fighting.

Teheran Bombardment

In recent weeks, the conflict has escalated further, with Tehran coming under bombardment. The attacks, which have been attributed to Israeli and US forces, have targeted Iranian military and industrial facilities, including nuclear research sites. The bombardment has been widely condemned by the international community, with many countries expressing concern about the potential consequences of the conflict.

Key Factors Driving the Conflict

Several key factors are driving the conflict between Iran and the Gulf states. One of the main drivers is the struggle for regional dominance, with Iran seeking to exert its influence over the Middle East and the Gulf states seeking to resist its advances. Another key factor is the sectarian divide between Shia Iran and the predominantly Sunni Gulf states, which has contributed to the escalation of tensions. Economic factors, including competition for energy resources and markets, have also played a significant role in the conflict.

Potential Implications

The conflict between Iran and the Gulf states has significant implications for regional and global stability. The fighting has already disrupted energy supplies, with oil prices rising in response to the attacks on Saudi and Emirati targets. The conflict also has the potential to draw in other regional actors, including Turkey and Israel, which could further escalate the situation. The international community has expressed concern about the potential consequences of the conflict, including the risk of a wider regional war and the potential for terrorism and extremism to spread.

US Role in the Conflict

The United States has played a significant role in the conflict, with the Trump administration having taken a hawkish stance towards Iran. The US has provided military support to the Gulf states, including the deployment of troops and equipment, and has imposed economic sanctions on Iran. The US has also been involved in the bombardment of Tehran, with American forces participating in the attacks on Iranian targets. The US role in the conflict has been widely criticized, with many countries expressing concern about the potential consequences of American involvement.

International Community Response

The international community has responded to the conflict with a mix of condemnation and calls for restraint. The United Nations has issued statements urging both sides to cease hostilities and engage in diplomatic efforts to resolve the conflict. The European Union has also called for calm, with EU leaders expressing concern about the potential consequences of the conflict. Other countries, including China and Russia, have taken a more nuanced approach, seeking to balance their relationships with both Iran and the Gulf states.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the conflict between Iran and the Gulf states continues to escalate, with no signs of abatement. The latest developments have seen a surge in Iranian attacks on the Gulf states, while Tehran itself has come under bombardment. The conflict has significant implications for regional and global stability, with the potential to draw in other actors and disrupt energy supplies. The international community must continue to call for restraint and engage in diplomatic efforts to resolve the conflict, while also addressing the underlying factors driving the tensions. The US role in the conflict has been widely criticized, and it remains to be seen how the new administration will approach the situation. One thing is certain, however: the conflict between Iran and the Gulf states will continue to be a major concern for regional and global stability in the years to come.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis presented in this report, several recommendations can be made. Firstly, the international community must continue to call for restraint and engage in diplomatic efforts to resolve the conflict.

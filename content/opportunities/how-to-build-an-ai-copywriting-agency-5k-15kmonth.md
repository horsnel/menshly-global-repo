## The Workflow: Step-by-Step With Every Shortcut

### Step 1: Intake & Quick‑Set (15 min)  
Grab a fresh Notion page, paste the client’s brief template, and hit Zapier to create a Trello card. Zapier pulls the form data straight into Trello, so the whole team can see the scope. Set a Calendly link in the email that auto‑schedules a 15‑minute kickoff call.  
Ask ChatGPT the following prompt:  
```
“Create a concise project brief outline for a 500‑word blog on [topic], targeting [persona] on a $xx price point. Include headline options, sub‑head ideas, and 3 key CTAs.”  
```
Copy the output, tweak the tone, and send the draft back to the client for instant approval.  
**💡 HACK:** Use Make.com to auto‑populate the Trello card with the ChatGPT output so you never copy‑paste manually.

### Step 2: Research & Persona Build (30 min)  
Run a quick Semrush keyword audit: pick 10 high‑volume, low‑competition terms. Export to CSV and use Make.com to push the list into Notion.  
Open Replit and drop a tiny script that scrapes the top 5 SERP pages for tone and structure. Feed the scraped content into ChatGPT with this prompt:  
```
“Analyze these 5 pages and summarize the top 3 copy patterns. Then generate a buyer persona named ‘Alex’ with buying triggers and objections.”  
```
Now you have a data‑driven persona and a keyword map.  
**💡 HACK:** Save the Replit script as a template; run it for every new brief and you’ll have 5‑minute keyword insights without manual copy‑pasting.

### Step 3: Draft, Polish & Visualize (1 h)  
Start ChatGPT in a new Replit session. Prompt:  
```
“Write a 500‑word blog post on [topic] for Alex. Use a conversational tone, include the keyword list, and embed 2 short bullet‑point facts.”  
```
Copy the output into Notion. Run Grammarly’s Chrome extension on the draft; it flags passive voice and awkward phrasing in real time.  
Open Canva, choose a pre‑made blog‑header template, and upload the headline. Canva’s “Brand Kit” syncs colors and fonts automatically.  
For the video version, paste the final text into Fliki AI. Set ElevenLabs voice to “en‑US‑Calvin” at 1.1x speed. Fliki auto‑generates subtitles and a thumbnail.  
Upload the PDF, video, and graphic assets to a shared folder in Hostinger, then generate a short landing page URL.  
**💡 HACK:** Use Zapier to push the completed Canva design to Buffer; set a schedule for the next month’s social posts and you’re done.

### Step 4: Delivery, Upsell & Retention (15 min)  
Record a quick Loom walkthrough of the deliverables, highlighting how the keywords boost SEO. Include a link to the landing page in the Loom caption.  
Send the Loom link plus a thank‑you note via Klaviyo. In the email, add a “next‑step” CTA: “Want a video version of this? Let’s talk.”  
Set up a follow‑up sequence in ActiveCampaign that triggers 5 days after delivery, asking for feedback and pitching a 20 % discount on the next project.  
Track the client’s engagement with Apollo.io’s email tracking; if opens are low, send a second reminder.  
**💡 HACK:** Use Hostinger’s free SSL to embed a short form on the landing page that auto‑feeds into Notion; you’ll have a lead capture stack that costs nothing.

With this workflow you’re spending no more than 2 hours per project, delivering high‑quality copy, and building a pipeline that can scale to $5 k+ per month. The key is automation, not hustle.

## What Nobody Warns You About

**Hidden Recurring Fees**  
You’ll think the free tier of Make.com is enough, but the 200‑operation/month cap shakes out fast when you start automating client workflows. Once you hit it, you’re looking at $39/month for the Pro plan, plus extra charges for each “advanced” action. The same happens with Replit. The $3/month Hobby plan is fine for prototypes, but a single script that pulls data from Apollo.io and pushes it into a Klaviyo email blast will eat up your credits, pushing you into the $10/month Pro tier. And don’t forget ChatGPT’s API: $0.02 per 1k tokens. A 1,000‑word landing page uses roughly 2,000 tokens, so that’s $0.04 per page. Multiply by 10 clients, and you’re already at $0.40 a month. These are the sneaky costs that turn a $5K/month profit into $3K when you forget to track them.  

**Quality vs. Speed Trap**  
Your first copy job might finish in 30 seconds with ChatGPT, but the polished, brand‑aligned version that a client pays for takes 2–3 hours of editing. If you only rely on the AI, you’ll underdeliver and undercharge. A realistic workflow uses Midjourney for visuals ($12/month), Canva for final design ($12/month), and Grammarly Premium ($12/month) to catch tone and tone‑shift errors. Add Loom for client walkthroughs ($8/month) and Buffer ($15/month) to schedule social posts. Expect to spend 6–8 hours per project if you’re doing high‑quality copy.  

**Client Expectations & Scope Creep**  
Clients love the promise of “instant copy.” Make it a clear contract: one headline, one CTA, one paragraph. Anything beyond that triggers an extra fee. Use Calendly ($0/month) to set up discovery calls and capture scope in Notion ($0). If a client asks for extra rewrites or new keywords, bill $100/hour or add a $500 retainer. Many copywriters go silent, thinking the AI makes it “free,” but the hidden labor still costs.  

**Legal & IP Pitfalls**  
AI models are trained on copyrighted text. If you feed a brand’s proprietary data into ChatGPT, the output may be flagged as derivative. The safest route is to run every draft through a plagiarism checker and keep a record of the original prompt. If you use Fliki AI to create a video, you’re allowed to use the voice only if you have the rights; ElevenLabs’s terms say you can’t sell voice clones without a license. Skipping these checks can land you in the mail from a copyright holder.

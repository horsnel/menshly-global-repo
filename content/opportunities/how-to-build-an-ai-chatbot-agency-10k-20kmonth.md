## What Nobody Warns You About

**The “Free” Plan Is a Mirage**  
You’ll start with Make.com’s free tier, Replit’s $7/month IDE, and Hostinger’s $3.99/month host. That looks like zero spend for the first month. But every time you hit the 5‑minute limit on Make.com or the 1 GB RAM cap on Replit, you pay $25/month for that extra capacity. Add in Shopify Basic $39/month and Zapier’s $19/month paid plan for real data transfers, and you’re looking at $88/month before you even launch a single client. Don’t forget Vapi’s $30/month voice layer and ElevenLabs’ $20/month voice synth if you want natural conversation. Those small “add‑ons” quickly add up, and many agencies think they’re optional until they’re already bleeding cash.

**Templates Are Cheap, Clients Are Not**  
You’ll see a lot of courses telling you to copy the same chatbot flow from a template and tweak it for each store. That works for a quick demo, but it turns every client into a “copy‑pasta” bot. A Shopify store that sells sneakers needs a different upsell logic than a dropship beauty site. If you don’t build a custom intent model in Replit and feed it specific SKUs, you’ll get a bot that mis‑answers 40% of the time. The result? A loss of $200/month per client in churn. Invest $300 a month in a custom NLP build or a paid LLM like GPT‑4 (API $0.03/1k tokens) to stay ahead.

**Clients Expect “Instant Success”**  
Most e‑commerce owners sign up hoping their new bot will double conversions overnight. In reality, you’ll need 3–4 weeks of data to train a voice model in ElevenLabs, and 6 weeks of conversational logs to fine‑tune a ChatGPT‑powered flow. Let them know that the first month is about data collection and setting metrics; the second month is about optimization. If you promise a 20% lift in sales the first week, you’ll break the client relationship and your reputation. Be blunt: “It takes 30 days to hit even a 5% lift.”

**Compliance Is a Legal Minefield**  
You’ll be handling customer data, chat logs, and payment questions. That means GDPR, CCPA, and PCI‑DSS compliance. Vapi and ElevenLabs store voice recordings; if you’re in the EU, you must get explicit consent and keep logs for 6 months. Forgetting to do so can cost up to $5 million in fines. Set up a Notion compliance checklist and schedule a quarterly audit with a lawyer. It feels like a detour, but it saves you from a lawsuit that could wipe out the agency in a single month.

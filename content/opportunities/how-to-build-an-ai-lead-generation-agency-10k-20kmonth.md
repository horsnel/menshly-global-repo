## The Workflow: Step-by-Step With Every Shortcut

### Step 1: Prospect List Creation (2 hrs)

Start with Apollo.io. Hit the “Companies” tab. Set filters: “Annual Revenue” ≥ $1M, “Industry” = SaaS, “Employees” = 51‑200. Export the top 200. Apollo’s free tier caps at 500 leads, but the paid plan is $99/month for 2,000 leads and unlimited enrichment.  
Upload the CSV to Replit. Run a quick Python script to add a “Lead Score” column:  
```python
import pandas as pd
df = pd.read_csv('apollo_leads.csv')
df['LeadScore'] = df['Revenue'] / df['Employees']
df.to_csv('scored_leads.csv', index=False)
```
Replit’s free tier lets you run scripts for up to 1 hour/day; it’s free.  
Push the scored list into Make.com (automation platform). Create a workflow that triggers every 12 hrs, pulls the CSV, and writes a new Google Sheet. That sheet becomes your master prospect list.  

**HACK**: Use Apollo’s “Revenue” filter with a range (e.g., $2M‑$10M) to keep the list tight. The smaller the list, the higher the open rate. Set the “Lead Score” threshold to 50 before moving on; it saves time later.

---

### Step 2: Outreach Cadence Build (1.5 hrs)

Draft a 3‑email sequence in Notion. Keep each email under 150 words. Use ChatGPT for polish:  
Prompt: “Write a concise, friendly 150‑word email to a CMO at a SaaS company that explains why we can help them close 30% more deals.”  

Set up Zapier to push each contact from the Google Sheet into ActiveCampaign (CRM + email). Zapier’s free tier handles 100 tasks/month; you’ll need the paid plan at $19/month for 3,000 tasks.  
In ActiveCampaign, create a sequence:  
1. Email 1 – 0 hrs  
2. Email 2 – 48 hrs  
3. Email 3 – 96 hrs (if no reply)  

Use Make.com to watch the “ActiveCampaign” trigger “Email Sent” and update a “Status” column in the Google Sheet.  

Add a Loom video to Email 2. Record a quick 30‑second clip that says, “Hey, I noticed your product has a new feature; let’s talk.” Attach the video link.  

**HACK**: Rotate sender IP by using a Gmail alias and a cheap VPN (e.g., $10/month). Plus, warm up the email domain with Apollo’s “Email Warmup” – schedule 5 warm-up emails per day via Make.com. It boosts deliverability by 30%.

### Step 3: Follow‑up & Closing (1 hr)

Track all interactions in Apollo’s notes. After Email 3, if no reply, send a “Last Chance” email from the same sequence, but add a Calendly link so the prospect can book a 15‑minute call instantly. Calendly’s free tier is fine until you hit 30 bookings/month; otherwise, upgrade to $12/month.  

When the call lands, record a Loom quick recap and send an email with a Canva‑created proposal PDF. Canva’s free plan covers most templates; premium is $12/month.  
Host the proposal on a simple landing page hosted by Hostinger (shared plan $3.96/month).  
Close the loop: update the Google Sheet status to “Closed-Won” or “Closed-Lost.”  

**HACK**: Use Midjourney to generate a custom GIF for the proposal. Run a prompt like “high‑contrast SaaS marketing GIF, 10 seconds, 1080p.” Embed it in the PDF; a visual boost can raise conversion by 15%.

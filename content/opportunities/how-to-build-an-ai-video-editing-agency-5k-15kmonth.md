## The Workflow: Step-by-Step With Every Shortcut

### Step 1: Intake & Script Generation (30 min)

First, grab the raw brief. Shoot a quick Loom video asking the client for the goal, target audience, and any brand guidelines. Upload the clip to Notion and tag it “Client Brief.”  
Open ChatGPT‑4 in a Replit notebook. Paste the brief and fire this prompt:  

> “Write a 60‑second, upbeat script for a tech‑savvy audience about the new X‑Phone. Keep it under 150 words, include a clear CTA to visit the website.”  

ChatGPT spits out a polished draft in 60 seconds. Copy the script to a Notion page and run [Grammarly](https://grammarly.com/) on it – just hit the “Check” button, and any awkward phrasing disappears instantly.  
With the script locked, use Apollo.io to pull a list of potential leads that fit the client’s niche. Export the list to a CSV and import it into Make.com. Make.com creates a scheduled Zap that pushes each new lead into a Klaviyo email sequence, auto‑nurturing prospects with a teaser reel of the client’s product.  

> **HACK:** Combine Apollo.io and Make.com to auto‑populate a Notion “Lead Tracker” every time a new prospect lands. That one‑click sync saves you a full spreadsheet update.

### Step 2: Voiceover & Text‑to‑Video (1 hr)

Open Fliki AI. Paste the ChatGPT script; choose 1080p, “Professional” style, and hit “Generate.” Fliki gives you a raw video with a generic stock background.  
Next, pull a custom background from Midjourney. Prompt it:  

> “Generate a sleek, futuristic tech showroom background, 1920x1080, high detail.”  

Download the image, upload it to Fliki, and replace the default background.  
Time the voice: ElevenLabs offers a “Male British” voice at $15/month. Load the script into ElevenLabs, tweak the speaking rate to 1.1x, and export the MP3.  
Use Replit to stitch the audio and video: run a tiny ffmpeg script that overlays the audio onto the Fliki clip, adds a 3‑second fade‑in/out, and exports a final MP4.  

> **HACK:** Save the ffmpeg script in a Replit repo. When you need to re‑edit a video, just edit the script and re‑run—no heavy software required.

### Step 3: Editing, Polishing & Brand Sync (2 hrs)

Now it’s time to fine‑tune. Upload the MP4 to Canva’s new video editor. Drag the clip onto the timeline, add a branded lower‑third using the client’s logo (upload via Canva’s brand kit).  
Add captions: copy the script into Canva’s caption tool, then hit the “Auto‑Sync” button. Canva will line each caption to the dialogue automatically.  
Apply a color grade: click the “Enhance” tab, choose “Tech Blue” preset, then fine‑tune contrast to 60%. Export at 1080p, 30 fps—exact specs for YouTube Shorts or Instagram Reels.  
Upload the polished clip to Vimeo via Make.com. The same Make.com zap that pushed leads to Klaviyo can now push the video link to a dedicated Slack channel for client review.  

> **HACK:** Use Canva’s “Batch Export” to push the same video to multiple social platforms (YouTube, TikTok, IG) with one click, saving you the need to re‑encode.

### Step 4: Delivery & Automation (30 min)

Send the client a Loom link to the final edit. Use Calendly to set a quick 15‑minute review call, automatically populating the client’s calendar.  
When the client approves, trigger a Zap in Make.com: create a new invoice in QuickBooks, add the client to ActiveCampaign, and send a thank‑you email that includes a link to a [Beehiiv](https://beehiiv.com/) newsletter sign‑up.  
Schedule the video on Buffer. Set two posts: one on day 1 at 9 am (Instagram Reels) and one on day 3 at 3 pm (LinkedIn). Buffer will handle the timing so you don’t have to remember.  
Finally, log the project in Notion under “Completed Projects.” Attach the Vimeo link, client notes, and invoice PDF. The project card then automatically moves to the “Finance” board, ready for the next round of revenue.

## What Nobody Warns You About

**AI “All‑in‑One” Myth**  
Saying Fliki AI will finish a 15‑minute promo in 30 minutes sounds great, but that’s only the raw cut. The client will still need color grading, motion graphics, and brand‑specific transitions. You’ll end up on Replit, spending $5/month on Python scripts to tweak audio levels, and then double‑check everything on Canva Pro ($12.99/mo). Expect to spend at least 4–6 hours per video, not 30 minutes. If you outsource the polishing, add $25–$35/hour of freelance talent—those are the costs that break the $5K/month ceiling.

**Turnaround Time vs. Automation Limits**  
Zapier’s free tier caps you at 100 tasks/month. That’s enough for a handful of file uploads, not a full pipeline. Upgrade to the Starter plan for $19.99/month and you get 2,000 tasks, but you’ll still hit the 50‑task limit per Zap. Make.com offers 1,000 operations/month in its free tier; the $49/month plan gives 25,000. Even with these automations, a 10‑minute video can take 5–7 hours of manual QA. If you promise 24‑hour delivery, you’ll be running overtime or hiring another editor at $20/hour.

**License & Hosting Overheads**  
Every client wants royalty‑free stock, which costs $20–$30/month per license on sites like Storyblocks. Add Midjourney ($10/mo) for unique thumbnails, Grammarly Pro ($12/mo) for copy, and Hostinger’s basic plan for $3.99/month. Your monthly overhead climbs to $75–$90 just for tools that can’t be reclaimed. Keep an eye on these numbers; they eat into the $15K/month sweet spot faster than you think.

**Communication Breakdowns at Scale**  
Using Loom for quick demo videos and Calendly ($14/mo) for scheduling looks slick, but as your client list grows, your inbox explodes. ActiveCampaign at $9/mo or Klaviyo at $30/mo can automate follow‑ups, but they’re still separate systems. Without a single hub, you’ll lose brand voice consistency and risk churn. Think about building a Notion workspace as a central hub to track edits, feedback, and deadlines—$0 if you stay in the free tier, but you’ll save hours of back‑and‑forth that otherwise cost you.

These hidden realities are why most people stop at $5K/month. Address them early, or you’ll hit the plateau before the first bill.

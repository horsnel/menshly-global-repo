## What Nobody Warns You About

**1. Hidden Cost of Scaling**  
You start with Make.com’s free tier, then drop into the $49/month Pro plan after 500 operations a month. That’s fine until you hit 10,000 customers—every 5,000 triggers another $49. Add Replit’s paid $7/month for GPU time, Vapi’s $29/mo per voice line, and Fliki AI’s $39/mo for high‑resolution video. The math hits $200/month just to keep the flow smooth. Plan ahead; a 10,000‑user launch can push you to $400–$500/month before you even consider adding a new feature.  

**2. Data Privacy Is a Tightrope Walk**  
Putting customer data into Klaviyo ($29/month) or ActiveCampaign ($29/month) is cheap, but you’re still responsible for GDPR and CCPA compliance. If you ship logs to a third‑party analytics tool like Semrush ($1,195/year), you’re handing over raw email addresses and behavioral data. That’s a liability you’ll pay for in a breach, not a feature. Make sure your server on Hostinger ($1.99/month) has encryption at rest, and that your AI voice synthesis with ElevenLabs ($9/month) never stores raw audio on your database.  

**3. Integration Hell Is Real**  
You’ll probably connect Zapier ($19.99/month) to funnel leads from Apollo.io ($99/month) into Shopify ($29/month) and then into Calendly ($10/month) for demos. Every time you add a new step, you add a single point of failure. A 1‑second lag in a Make.com workflow can push a 3‑minute onboarding flow to 4 minutes, and customers abandon. Keep an eye on latency; a simple buffer (Buffer $12/month) or a debug log in Notion ($8/month) can catch it before it kills you.  

**4. The “Free” Tools Are Not Free**  
Canva Pro ($12.95/month) and Midjourney ($10/month) give you fresh assets, but every use of their API counts toward a monthly quota. If you ask a customer to upload a photo and then run it through Midjourney for a personalized avatar, that’s a $0.02 line item you didn’t budget for. Grammarly ($12/month) only fixes 5,000 words a month on the free plan; beyond that you hit $12/month per user. When you scale to 1,000 users, those micro‑costs explode into hundreds of dollars, and the margin evaporates.  

Stick to a strict budget, audit every API call, and remember that the cheapest option today can be the most expensive tomorrow.

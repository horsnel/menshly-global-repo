## The Workflow: Step-by-Step With Every Shortcut

### Step 1: Prospect List Creation (1 – 2 hrs)

Grab a fresh Notion table. Build columns: Company, Domain, Contact Name, Title, Email, LinkedIn URL, Lead Score, Notes.  
Open Apollo.io’s free plan, pull 500 leads that match “SaaS founders” and export to CSV. Import that CSV straight into Notion.  
Use a quick Make.com scenario: trigger on new Notion row, run a [Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/) domain audit, append Domain Authority and traffic numbers.  
Set a Lead Score threshold of 60 to keep only high‑intent prospects.  
Filter out any duplicates or spammy domains.  
You’ll have a clean, enriched list of 350 prospects in under two hours. That’s the foundation of any cold‑email machine.

⚡ HACK:  
- In Apollo, use “Prospect Search” filters for “Company Size > 10” and “Title ∈ Founder, CEO.”  
- When importing to Notion, use the “Import CSV” block instead of manual copy‑paste; it preserves formatting and saves 10 min.  

### Step 2: Copy Generation & Personalization (45 min)

Open ChatGPT on the new $20/month plan. Feed it the Notion table as a prompt:  
> “I want 10 subject lines and 5 email bodies for each of these 350 prospects. Each email must mention the company’s recent product launch and propose a 15‑min demo.”  
Copy the whole block, paste into a new Notion page, and let ChatGPT spin each email with a unique angle.  
Run each email through [Grammarly](https://grammarly.com/) (free) to catch passive voice and over‑complex sentences.  
Add a personal image using Midjourney: prompt “professional headshot of a tech founder in a boardroom.” Save the image, then embed it into the email body with Canva’s free design tools.  
You now have 1,750 personalized emails ready to send. All in under 45 minutes.

⚡ HACK:  
- Use the ChatGPT “Custom Instruction” feature to keep tone consistent: “Answer like a friendly sales rep who loves clean, concise copy.”  
- In Midjourney, lock the style with “–style corporate” to get uniform headshots and save time on manual edits.  

### Step 3: Deliverability & Sending Pipeline (30 min)

Create an email account on Hostinger for $3.99/month; set up DKIM, SPF, and DMARC for maximum inbox penetration.  
Use Make.com to automate the pipeline: trigger on new Notion row → format email → upload image to Canva → send via ActiveCampaign (free tier allows 1,000 contacts).  
Set ActiveCampaign to send a single email per day per domain to avoid throttling.  
Add a “Send” button that calls [Vapi](https://vapi.ai/)’s AI voice agent; Vapi will read the email aloud for you to double‑check tone before sending.  
Run a quick ping test with SendGrid’s free tier to ensure deliverability metrics are above 95%.

⚡ HACK:  
- In Make.com, use the “Delay” module to stagger sends by 2‑minute intervals; this keeps you under the daily limit for most free tiers.  
- Configure ActiveCampaign’s “Spam Score” to < 20 by removing any marketing tags

## What Nobody Warns You About

**Spam’s Silent Killer**  
Short: Most people think a generic template will work.  
Long: In reality, 67% of inboxes filter plain text into spam because they lack real personalization. **Data Costs Are Not Included**  
Short: Lead lists aren’t free.  
Long: Apollo.io starts at $99/month for 50,000 contacts, but the “Pro” tier that gives you verified emails costs $299/month. Add a Semrush domain audit ($99.95/month) for each prospect to verify authority. Short: Mixing tools can trip you up.  
Long: Make.com’s free tier lets you run 500 operations per month, but once you hit 5,000 you’re at $29/month. Zapier is $19.99/month for 2,000 tasks; they don’t talk about the 30‑second latency when you layer both. A Replit script that pulls from Apollo and pushes into Klaviyo can eat up 15 minutes per run if it hits a rate limit. Every 30 seconds you’re losing $0.25 in potential outreach, so think about a single platform that covers most of the workflow instead of juggling 8.

**Pricing Strategy Is a Minefield**  
Short: Underpricing kills the business.  
Long: Many solo founders start at $500/month, assuming it’s “cheap” for clients.

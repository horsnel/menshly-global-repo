## Opening Hook
You’d think 2026 would bring headline‑grabbing tech, not a service that lets you write legal contracts in 30 seconds. Yet here it is. A single high‑quality contract takes a lawyer eight hours, but an LLM can spit out a first draft in half a minute. If you can turn that raw text into a polished, client‑ready document in under ten minutes, you’re looking at a market that pays $5‑$25 k per month.

The shock? The pipeline. In 2025, the legal tech market was already $9.5 billion; it’s projected to hit $34 billion by 2030. Every one of those dollars is a potential client who wants a cheaper, faster alternative to the old‑school law firm. I’m going to lay out everything: the exact tools, the tricks nobody shares, the ugly truths, and the realistic numbers.

## Why This Works Right Now
1. **LLMs Are Economical** – GPT‑4‑Turbo and Claude‑3 have become affordable. The cost per 1,000 tokens is under $0.01 on OpenAI’s edge tier. That means you can write dozens of contracts for the price of a coffee. In the past, law firms charged $250‑$500 per hour; now you can do the same work for a fraction of that cost.  
2. **Demand for Rapid Prototyping** – Startups, freelancers, and small businesses need documents fast. They’re willing to pay $200‑$500 for a contract that’s ready to send, not $2,500 for a lawyer’s retainer. The gap is ripe.  
3. **Automation Platforms are Mature** – Zapier, Make.com, and [Replit](https://replit.com/refer/egwuokwor) let you glue together AI, design, email, and CRM tools without hiring a dev. The only overhead is learning the workflow.

## The Workflow: Step‑by‑Step With Every Shortcut  
### Step 1: Capture Lead & Intake (10 min)  
- Client lands on a Hostinger‑hosted landing page.  
- They fill a simple form: name, email, project type.  
- Zapier (Professional plan) pushes data into Notion as a new “Lead” page.  
- ActiveCampaign auto‑sends a “Hi, we’ve got your request” email with a short questionnaire.  
**HACK:** Use Make.com to auto‑populate the questionnaire with default answers based on project type; you’ll get the first draft in 3 minutes instead of waiting for the client to reply.  

### Step 2: Draft Contract (15 min)  
- Your Replit app pulls the client’s answers from Notion, builds a prompt:  
  ```  
  Draft a non‑disclosure agreement for a freelance web developer working for a SaaS startup in San Francisco, with a 12‑month term and $50,000 total fee.  
  ```  
- ChatGPT Plus returns a raw contract in 30 seconds.  
- Grammatical check via Grammarly (free) fixes minor errors.  
- Canva Pro templates format the PDF with your brand colors.  
**HACK:** In Make.com, add a “Token‑budget” node that trims the ChatGPT prompt to 1,000 tokens if you’re near your limit. This keeps costs under $0.01 per contract.  

### Step 3: Review & Personalize (10 min)  
- You review the contract on Notion’s “Draft” page.  
- Use the built‑in Notion comments to add a “Sign‑off” flag.  
- If you’re a solo founder, you can approve yourself; if you have a lawyer, you’ll forward the link to their Notion page.  
- Once approved, the contract is moved to “Ready” folder.  
- Make.com triggers a signed‑off PDF to ActiveCampaign, which sends a “Your contract is ready” email with a link to download.  
**HACK:** Add a “Signature block” generator that pulls the client’s name and date automatically using Replit’s string formatting.  

### Step 4: Follow‑Up & Upsell (5 min)  
- After the client downloads, a Loom video auto‑plays explaining the contract sections.  
- ActiveCampaign schedules a follow‑up email after 48 hours asking if they need additional clauses.  
- If they reply “Yes,” Zapier pulls up a new prompt: “Add a jurisdiction clause for Canada to the existing NDA.”  
- ChatGPT returns the updated section instantly.  
**HACK:** Use ElevenLabs to synthesize the Loom video’s audio, adding a professional voiceover that reads the contract summary.

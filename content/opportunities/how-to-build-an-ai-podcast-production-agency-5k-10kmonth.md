## What Nobody Warns You About

**1. The Myth of Zero Setup Cost**  
You can start with a free Replit account to prototype scripts, but the first $0 you spend on a tool usually turns into a subscription. Replit’s paid tier is $15/month for unlimited private repo, faster CPUs, and GPU access. Make.com’s automation starts at $39/month if you need to trigger 25,000 tasks a month. I cut the first month of the $15 Replit plan, but the project hit the free tier cap after a week. Don’t forget the hidden charge for a domain: Hostinger’s cheapest plan is $2.99/month, but you’ll need a custom domain to look legit. Add a Canva Pro subscription at $12/month for brand‑consistent thumbnails. The total hidden monthly cost can climb to $50 before you even pitch a client.  

**2. Your AI Voice Credits Burn Faster Than You Think**  
Vapi’s $99/month plan gives you 10,000 voice minutes. ElevenLabs charges $0.02 per minute for advanced neural voices. If you produce a 30‑minute episode, you’re already at $0.60 per episode. Multiply that by 5 episodes a week, and you’re burning $12.50 a week just on voice. Most people ignore the fact that the “free” tier of ElevenLabs caps at 200 minutes per month. When you hit the limit, you’re forced to upgrade mid‑production, throwing off deadlines. Expect to pay at least $15/month for voice synthesis, or you’ll be scrambling to cut content.  

**3. Client Perception vs Reality**  
Clients think AI means “no human touch.” They’ll ask for “quick turnaround” and “cheap rates.” A $2,500 season contract hides the fact that you’ll be spending $500 on the paid tiers of Apollo.io for outreach, $50/month on Buffer for social promotion, and $20/month on Loom for demos. You’ll also need a $99/month Klaviyo plan to handle follow‑up emails. That’s an extra $3,100/month of overhead you can’t show on a quote sheet. If you don’t explain the cost structure, you’ll be overpromising and underdelivering, and you’ll pay the price in churn.  

**4. Scaling the Workflow Isn’t Free**  
Automating with Zapier starts at $19.99/month for 100 tasks, but most podcasts need 200+ tasks a month for transcriptions, social posts, and email triggers. Switching to Make.com’s paid tier at $39/month gives you 25,000 tasks, but that’s still a line item you can’t ignore. You’ll also need a $25/month Notion plan for premium collaboration and a $39/month ActiveCampaign plan for advanced CRM. The scaling cost can jump from $200/month to $500/month within a month. Expect to invest at least $700/month in tools before you hit the $5,000‑plus profit mark.

## What Nobody Warns You About

**1. “Free” Tools Are a Mirage**  
You’ll think you can build a full service on free tiers. That’s the biggest lie. Make.com’s free plan caps you at 500 operations/month; push beyond, and it costs $19/month. **2. Hidden Costs of Data Feeds**  
Semrush starts at $119.95/month for the Pro plan, but that includes 10,000 keyword positions. If you want deep crawl data for 5,000 websites, you’re looking at $199/month. These add up until you’re flinging the whole stack into production—don’t wait until a client sees a lagged report and asks why it’s slow.

**3. Client Expectations vs. Reality**  
Clients think you’ll drop them from Rank 200 to Rank 3 overnight. That’s a myth. The first 3 months are mainly auditing and keyword research. Even with an AI‑driven on‑page checker, the algorithm lift takes 4–6 weeks. Many agencies pivot to “quick wins” like local SERP features; you’ll be using Apollo.io’s $19/month B2B lead list for local businesses, but you’ll still need 20–30 hours of follow‑up. If you promise instant traffic and deliver a 2‑page case study, you’ll lose credibility.

**4. Scaling Requires More Than Tech**  
Your automation stack looks sleek, but scaling means hiring an analyst. A junior SEO analyst earns $35–$45/hour. Outsourcing to freelancers via Upwork adds a 30% overhead. Canva Pro costs $12.99/month per designer, Midjourney $10/month per account, and Grammarly Premium $30/month per writer. When you add activeCampaign’s $9/month for 500 contacts, the monthly spend climbs past $2,500 before you hit $10,000 revenue. Plan the burn rate, not just the cost of tools.

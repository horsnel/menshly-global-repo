## What Nobody Warns You About

**1. Automation Is A Double‑Edged Sword**  
You’ll think Zapier or Make.com will instantly fire listings to every channel, but the first 24 hours of a new workflow are a spam minefield. Each trigger in Zapier’s paid tier costs $20/month, and if you’re pushing 200 leads a day, that’s $7.30 per thousand contacts. A single mis‑configured step can flag your domain in Gmail’s filter, making every email bounce and costing you a $49 per month Hostinger plan. The short answer: test one lead at a time, then double. The long answer: every automation loop has a failure mode that can trash your sender reputation.

**2. Data Quality Is Cheaper Than Re‑Training**  
Apollo.io gives you 10,000 B2B contacts a month for $99, but a single typo in a company name can turn a source of cold calls into a dead‑end. No amount of GPT‑4 prompt tweaking will fix a malformed address. Scrape with PhantomBuster only if you scrub the output with [Grammarly](https://grammarly.com/)’s 30‑day free trial before sending. In practice, 15% of scraped leads are invalid, and cleaning that batch adds at least a $15/hour developer or a $200/month Replit subscription to your costs. If you ignore this, you’ll spend more on API calls than on actual outreach.

**3. Scaling Costs Grow Exponentially**  
Your first month you might run everything on a free Replit plan, but as you add 20 agents each needing a separate AI voice from ElevenLabs at $0.01 per second, the bill spikes to $180/month in 3 months. Add Fliki AI for video tours ($15/month per account) and Canva Pro ($12.99/month) and you’re already past $250/month for tooling you thought was “free.” The real kicker: every new client adds a host on Hostinger, a Shopify store ($29/month) and a Klaviyo email flow ($20/month). Plan for a 30% incremental cost whenever you add a client, or you’ll hit cash flow crunches before hitting $10k/month.

**4. No AI Can Replace Human Trust**  
You might think a Vapi voice agent can answer every question, but prospects still want a real human to negotiate terms. The first time a buyer says “I need to talk to the owner,” your automated funnel will auto‑dial a bot. The bot will politely say “I’m sorry, I’m not available.” That’s a lost sale. A 20‑minute Loom video review session with a real agent costs you a $50/hour rate, but it keeps clients from bouncing. The nuance: automate the repetitive, but keep the high‑touch moments in the human loop. Otherwise, your churn rate will climb faster than you can afford to replace that time.

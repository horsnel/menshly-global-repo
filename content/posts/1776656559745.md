<p>Naked mole rats are highly social, burrowing rodents that live in large colonies with a strict caste system. When the queen dies or is overthrown, the colony is thrown into a state of turmoil as the remaining mole rats engage in bloody wars of succession to determine the new queen. This process is crucial for the survival of the colony, as the new queen will play a vital role in shaping the colony's social structure and ensuring its continued prosperity.</p>

<p>In a typical naked mole rat colony, the process of succession is marked by intense fighting and competition among the female mole rats. When the queen dies or is overthrown, the colony's social structure is disrupted, and the female mole rats begin to compete for the top spot. This competition can be fierce, with females engaging in intense battles that can result in serious injury or even death.

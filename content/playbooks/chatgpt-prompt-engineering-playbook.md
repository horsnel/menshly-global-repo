## Sample Excerpt

### The RC-TCF Framework: Write Prompts That Work Every Time

Most people write prompts like search queries: "write me a blog post about AI in healthcare." Then they're disappointed when the output is generic. Professional prompt engineering uses a structured framework that eliminates ambiguity and gives the AI everything it needs to produce excellent output.

**R — Role:** Who should the AI be? "You are a healthcare technology journalist with 10 years of experience writing for hospitals and health systems."

**C — Context:** What's the situation? "A mid-size regional hospital is evaluating AI tools for patient triage. The CMO is skeptical because a previous vendor overpromised and underdelivered."

**T — Task:** What exactly should the AI do? "Write a 1,200-word blog post that explains AI patient triage realistically, acknowledging limitations while making the case for a pilot program."

**C — Constraints:** What are the boundaries? "No hype. Include at least 3 specific examples of hospitals that have implemented triage AI. Use a measured, professional tone. Avoid jargon that a CMO wouldn't know."

**F — Format:** How should the output be structured? "Use H2 headers. Include a 'Key Takeaways' box at the top. End with a clear next-steps section."

Here's the difference. The vague prompt produces a 600-word generic overview. The RC-TCF prompt produces a 1,200-word, audience-specific, publication-ready piece that requires minimal editing. Same AI. Different prompt. That's the engineering in prompt engineering.

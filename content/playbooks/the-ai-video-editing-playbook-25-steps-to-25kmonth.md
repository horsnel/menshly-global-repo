## Procedure 1.1: Register Your Business Domain on Hostinger

1. **Open your web browser** and go to the Hostinger domain search page:  
   <https://www.hostinger.com/domains>  
   *Wait until the page fully loads (the search bar should be centered).*

2. In the search bar, **type your desired domain** (e.g., *ai-videoedit.com*) and click the **Search** button (bolded).  
   *The results page should list the domain name with a price next to it.*

3. If the domain is available, click the **Add to Cart** button (bolded).  
   *You should see a modal popup confirming the domain was added.*

4. In the popup, click **Proceed to Checkout** (bolded).  
   *The checkout page should display the domain name and price.*

5. **Create a Hostinger account** or log in:  
   - Click **Create an Account** (bolded).  
   - Fill in **Name**, **Email**, **Password** (choose 12‑char minimum, mix of letters & numbers).  
   - Click **Create Account** (bolded).  
   *You should receive a confirmation email.*

6. **Verify your email** by opening the Hostinger email, clicking **Verify**.  
   *Your account dashboard should now load.*

7. Return to the domain checkout page (refresh if needed).  
   *The domain should still be in your cart.*

8. **Enter your billing information**:  
   - Credit card number, expiry, CVV.  
   - **Billing address** must match the card.  
   - Click **Submit** (bolded).  
   *A confirmation screen with “Order Placed” should appear.*

9. **Do you see the domain listed in your Hostinger dashboard?**  
   If not, go to **My Domains** → **Add Domain** → re-enter the domain.  
   *The domain should now appear under “Active Domains.”*

10. **Set up domain privacy**:  
    - In the domain details, click **Privacy** → **Enable Privacy** (bolded).  
    - Confirm with **Yes, Enable** (bolded).  
    *You should see “Privacy: Enabled” on the domain page.*

11. **Configure DNS settings**:  
    - Click **DNS** → “Use Hostinger’s default DNS” (toggle ON).  
    - Save changes.  
    *The DNS status should read “Active.”*

12. **Validate domain registration**:  
    - Open a new tab and go to <https://whois.domaintools.com/>.  
    - Enter your domain, click **Search** (bolded).  
    - Verify that the registrar is Hostinger and the creation date is correct.  
    *Expected output: WHOIS record showing Hostinger as registrar.*

13. **Do you see the WHOIS record with Hostinger?**  
    If the registrar shows “Not found,” the domain may still be propagating.  
    *Wait 30 minutes and refresh.*

14. **Set up a sub‑domain for your Loom channel**:  
    - In Hostinger dashboard → **Subdomains** → **Add Subdomain**.  
    - Enter **loom** as the subdomain and select your domain.  
    - Click **Create** (bolded).  
    *You should see “loom.ai-videoedit.com” listed.*

15. **Verify the sub‑domain**:  
    - Open <https://loom.ai-videoedit.com> in a browser.  
    - The page should display a 404 with “Page not found.”  
    *This confirms the sub‑domain is active.*

16. **Optional — Auto‑renewal**:  
    - Go to **Billing** → **Auto‑Renew** → toggle ON for your domain.  
    - Confirm with **Enable Auto‑Renew** (bolded).  
    *You should see “Auto‑Renew: Enabled.”*

17. **Error scenario**:  
    - **If you see “Domain already registered”** when you try to add it, this means the domain was previously purchased by another account.  
      **Fix it by**:  
      a) Searching for an alternative domain (add a keyword).  
      b) Contact Hostinger support via live chat, provide the domain name, and request a transfer or alternative suggestion.

18. **Record domain details**:  
    - Open [**Notion**](https://notion.so/) → Create a new page titled “Domain Info.”  
    - Copy the domain name, registration date, renewal date, and privacy status into a table.  
    *This table will serve as a reference for future audits.*

19. **Share domain ownership with your AI video editing team**:  
    - In Hostinger dashboard → **Account Settings** → **Add User**.  
    - Enter the teammate’s email, select **Domain Manager** role, and click **Add** (bolded).  
    *They should receive an invitation email.*

20. **Close the procedure**:  
    - Bookmark <https://www.hostinger.com/domains> for quick future checks.  
    - Keep the confirmation email and dashboard screenshot for your records.

## Procedure 1.2: Set Up Email Forwarding in Hostinger

1. **Open your web browser** and navigate to the Hostinger login page:  
   <https://my.hostinger.com/login>  
   *Enter your Hostinger credentials (email and password) and click the **Login** button.*

2. **After logging in** you will land on the Hostinger Dashboard. In the left‑hand sidebar, click the **Email** icon (it looks like a paper envelope).  
   *The Email Overview page should load, showing a list of your hosted domains.*

3. Click the **Manage** button next to the domain you want to forward email for (e.g., `yourbusiness.com`).  
   *You should now see the Email Settings screen for that domain.*

4. In the top menu of the Email Settings screen, click **Email Forwarders**.  
   *A table of existing forwarders (if any) should appear.*

   **Check‑in #1**  
   Do you see the “Email Forwarders” tab and a table of forwarders?  
   If not, ensure you selected the correct domain and that you have an email account created for that domain.

5. Click the **Add Forwarder** button (bolded).  
   *A modal window titled “Add Email Forwarder” appears.*

6. In the **Source Email** field, type the email address you want to forward from, e.g., `info@yourbusiness.com`.  
   *The field should auto‑complete if the address exists.*

7. In the **Destination Email** field, type the external email address you want the messages forwarded to, e.g., `you@personalmail.com`.  
   *This is the address that will receive the forwarded emails.*

8. Click the **Create Forwarder** button (bolded).  
   *The new forwarder will appear in the table with a green check‑mark.*

   **Check‑in #2**  
   Do you see your new forwarder listed with a green check‑mark?  
   If you receive “Error: Invalid email address,” double‑check the formatting of both addresses (no spaces, correct domain).

9. Return to the Hostinger Dashboard. In the left sidebar, click **Automation** → [**Make.com**](https://www.make.com/en/register?pc=menshly) (Hostinger’s integration with Make.com).  
   *The Make.com dashboard opens in a new tab.*

10. Click the **Connect** button next to the “Email Forwarder” trigger (bolded).  
    *A pop‑up will request permission to access your Hostinger account.*

11. In the pop‑up, enter your Hostinger credentials again and click **Allow Access**.  
    *You should be redirected back to the Make.com workflow editor.*

12. Drag the **Send Email** action from the task library into the workflow canvas.  
    *You’ll see a node labeled “Send Email” connected to the trigger.*

13. Click the **Send Email** node to configure it.  
    *In the right‑hand panel, set “To” to `you@personalmail.com`, “Subject” to `New Forwarded Email`, and “Body” to `A new message was forwarded from {{Source Email}}.`*

14. Click the **Save** button (bolded) at the top of the page.  
    *The workflow should now be active.*

   **Check‑in #3**  
   Do you see the workflow with a green “Active” status?  
   If the status is “Paused,” click the toggle to activate it.

15. Open a new tab and go to your external email provider (e.g., Gmail). Send a test email to `info@yourbusiness.com`.  
    *You should receive the forwarded email within seconds.*

16. In Hostinger, navigate back to **Email Forwarders** and click the **Edit** button (pencil icon) next to the forwarder.  
    *The edit modal opens.*

17. In the **Destination Email** field, replace `you@personalmail.com` with a new address, e.g., `client@clientmail.com`.  
    *Click **Update Forwarder** (bolded).*

18. Verify the change by sending another test email to `info@yourbusiness.com`.  
    *The email should now arrive at the updated destination address.*

   **Check‑in #4**  
   Do you see the updated destination in the forwarder list?  
   If the email still arrives at the old address, clear your email client’s cache and resend.

19. **Optional: Add a Spam Filter**  
    In the Email Settings page, click **Spam Filter** (middle tab).  
    *Set “Block Emails From” to `*@spamdomain.com` and click **Save**.*

20. **Final Check**  
    Send a final test email to `info@yourbusiness.com`.  
    *You should receive it at the updated destination and it should not be flagged as spam.*

   **Check‑in #5**  
   Do you see the test email in the inbox of the destination address?  
   If not, double‑check the forwarder table and ensure the workflow in Make.com is active.

   - D1: `Expenses ($)`  
   - E1: `Profit ($)`

     - G1: `Tier`  
     - H1: `Price ($)`  
     - I1: `Deliverables`  
     - J1: `Margin (%)`  
   - Row 2: `Basic` | `250` | `1‑minute edited clip, 1 revision` | `60`  
   - Row 3: `Standard` | `500` | `3‑minute edited clip, 2 revisions, intro/outro` | `70`  
   - Row 4: `Premium` | `1000` | `5‑minute edited clip, 3 revisions, custom intro, music license` | `80`  

*Check‑in:* Do you see the tier table with correct pricing and margin percentages? If the margins look off, ensure numbers are entered exactly as shown.  

7. **Calculate Revenue per Tier**  
   - In cell L1, type `Revenue per Tier`  
   - In L2, enter: `=G2*H2` (this multiplies clients by price)  
   - Drag the formula down to L4.  

*Check‑in:* Does column L show `250`, `500`, `1000` for each tier? If not, verify the formula references.

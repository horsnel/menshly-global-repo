## What You'll Need

**Tools:** Make.com account (Teams plan at $16/month recommended for multi-user and error handling), OpenAI API key ($5–$50/month for typical workloads), access to the apps you want to integrate (Google Workspace, Slack, HubSpot, etc.), and a testing environment. Optional: n8n self-hosted as a backup for clients who prefer on-premise.

**Budget:** $50–$100/month for your own tooling. Client workflows may require additional API costs, which you pass through with a 30–50% markup. No significant upfront investment needed.

**Skills:** Logical thinking and comfort with visual workflow builders. Make.com is entirely no-code, but you need to understand data structures (JSON), API basics (webhooks, authentication), and conditional logic. If you can build a complex Excel formula, you can build a Make.com workflow.

**Time investment:** 2–3 weeks to become proficient. Build 5 practice workflows before taking on paid work. Each workflow teaches you patterns — error handling, data transformation, rate limiting — that compound into expertise.

## Step-by-Step Implementation

### Step 1: Master the 5 Core Patterns

Every business automation is a combination of five patterns. Learn these, and you can build anything:

1. **Data Sync:** Moving data between systems (new lead in Facebook Ads → create contact in HubSpot → send Slack notification)
2. **Content Generation:** AI-powered creation (new row in Google Sheets → GPT-5 generates email → sends via Gmail → logs in CRM)
3. **Monitoring and Alert:** Watching for conditions (competitor price change → AI analysis → Telegram alert with recommended response)
4. **Data Enrichment:** Enhancing existing records (new lead email → Clearbit enrichment → GPT-5 scores lead quality → routes to appropriate sales rep)
5. **Multi-Step Orchestration:** Chaining the above patterns into complex business processes

Build one workflow for each pattern as practice. Publish them to your portfolio.

### Step 2: Build Your Automation Catalog

Create 10 ready-to-deploy automation templates targeting common business pain points. These aren't generic — they're vertical-specific with real business logic. Examples:

- **E-commerce:** Abandoned cart → personalized AI email → discount offer → recovery tracking
- **Real Estate:** New listing → AI-generated property description → publish to 4 platforms → social media posts
- **SaaS:** Trial signup → 5-email onboarding sequence (AI-personalized) → usage monitoring → upgrade nudge
- **Professional Services:** New inquiry → AI qualification chat → booking → intake form → CRM entry → welcome email

Each template should be deployable in under 2 hours with client-specific customization.

### Step 3: Package and Price

Structure your offering in three tiers:

- **Quick Automate ($500–$1,000):** Single workflow, up to 5 modules, deployed in 48 hours
- **Process Automate ($2,000–$4,000):** Connected workflow system, 3–5 related automations, deployed in 2 weeks
- **Enterprise Automate ($5,000–$15,000):** Full business process redesign, 10+ workflows, ongoing optimization

Add monthly management at 15–20% of setup cost for monitoring, bug fixes, and iteration.

### Step 4: Client Acquisition Through "Automation Audits"

Offer free 30-minute automation audits. Screen-share with the prospect as they walk through their daily workflows. Identify 3–5 automation opportunities and present a proposal on the call. Close rate for this approach: 40–50% because the value is visible in real-time.

### Step 5: Delivery and Quality Assurance

Every workflow goes through this QA process before go-live: (1) Test with realistic data volumes, (2) Test error scenarios (API failures, missing data, rate limits), (3) Add error handlers and fallback paths, (4) Create a "runbook" document explaining the workflow for the client, (5) Monitor for 48 hours post-launch before handing off.

### Step 6: Build Recurring Revenue

Management retainers are where the real money lives. Clients who pay for setup are 70% likely to also pay for management if you frame it correctly: "Automation is like a garden — it needs tending as your business evolves." Include monthly optimization reviews, performance reports, and one workflow modification per month.

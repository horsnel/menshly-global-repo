You are going to build an AI course creation business. Not a blog about courses. Not a YouTube channel about teaching. A business that takes your expertise, turns it into structured educational products using AI, and sells those products for money. This guide covers every step. Follow it in order. Do not skip steps.

- A laptop with a modern browser and webcam
- A ChatGPT Plus account ($20/mo) — go to chat.openai.com

Total upfront cost: $20 for ChatGPT Plus.

## Step 1: Validate Your Course Topic

Before creating anything, validate that people will pay for your course. This step saves you from the most common course creator mistake: building something nobody wants.

### The 4-Criteria Scoring Framework

Open a Google Sheet. Create columns: Topic, Market Demand (1-5), Willingness to Pay (1-5), Competition Level (1-5, where 5 = low competition), Your Credibility (1-5), Total Score.

Score potential topics. Topics scoring 14+ are worth pursuing.

### Validate with Google Trends

Go to trends.google.com. Search your topic keyword. If interest is rising or stable over 12 months, demand exists. If declining, the market is moving on.

### Check Existing Courses

Go to udemy.com and search your topic. If there are 3-7 courses with 1,000+ students each, that is proven demand with room for a better product. If 50+ courses, the market is saturated. If zero courses, that is a red flag.

### Run a Pre-Sale Test

Create a landing page on Carrd (free) with the course title, 5 learning outcomes, and a "Join Waitlist" button. Drive 100 visitors through Twitter or LinkedIn. If 3-5 people sign up, you have demand. If 0, pivot.

### CHECK-IN: Step 1 Complete

1. Topic scored 14+ on the 4-criteria framework
2. Google Trends shows rising or stable interest
3. Udemy has 3-7 competing courses (proven demand)
4. Pre-sale landing page generated 3+ waitlist signups

## Step 2: Create the Curriculum with AI

### Generate the Course Outline

Open ChatGPT. Use this prompt:

> "I am creating an online course titled '[Your Course Title]' for [target audience]. The course should take [X hours] to complete and be priced at $[price]. Generate a detailed curriculum with: 1) 5-7 modules with clear learning objectives, 2) 3-4 lessons per module with specific topics, 3) One practical exercise per module, 4) A final project that demonstrates mastery. Format as a structured outline."

### Edit Ruthlessly

Remove any module that does not directly contribute to the student's transformation. Every lesson must answer: "What will the student be able to DO after this lesson?"

### Write Lesson Scripts

For each lesson, use this prompt:

> "Write a 5-8 minute lesson script for '[Lesson Title]' in the course '[Course Title]'. The student is [target audience]. Include: (1) An engaging opening that states what they will learn, (2) The core content with specific examples and data points, (3) A practical exercise they can complete immediately, (4) A transition to the next lesson. Write in a conversational but authoritative tone."

Edit each script for accuracy and personality. Add your own examples and insights.

### CHECK-IN: Step 2 Complete

1. Curriculum outline with 5-7 modules, 3-4 lessons each
2. Every lesson has a learning objective and practical exercise
3. All lesson scripts written and edited (5-8 minutes each)

## Step 3: Produce the Course Content

### Production Pipeline (Per Lesson)

1. **Write the script** using ChatGPT (refined with your expertise)
2. **Create a slide deck** in Canva AI (use presentation template, keep slides visual)
3. **Record the lesson** — Loom for screen-share walkthroughs, Synthesia ($22/mo) for AI-avatar lectures
4. **Edit** in Descript ($24/mo) — remove filler words, add captions, trim dead air
5. **Create a worksheet** in Google Docs — one actionable deliverable per lesson

Quality standard: each lesson 5-15 minutes. Shorter is better.

### The AI Production Sprint

Here is how to produce a 20-lesson course in one weekend:

**Saturday morning:** Generate all 20 lesson scripts using ChatGPT (2-3 hours). Edit for accuracy and personality (1 hour).

**Saturday afternoon:** Create slide decks in Canva AI for all 20 lessons (2 hours). Record all 20 lessons using Loom (3-4 hours).

**Sunday morning:** Edit all recordings in Descript — remove filler words, add captions (3 hours).

**Sunday afternoon:** Create worksheets for each module (1 hour). Build the course on Teachable (1 hour). Write the sales page (1 hour).

### CHECK-IN: Step 3 Complete

1. All lessons recorded with clear audio and visual slides
2. All filler words removed and captions added
3. Worksheets created for each module
4. Course structure uploaded to Teachable

## Step 4: Build and Launch on Teachable

### Create Your School

Go to teachable.com. Sign in. Click **Create a New School**. Name it. Choose the free plan.

### Create Your Course

Click **Courses** → **New Course**. Enter the course title. Add all modules and lessons in order. Upload videos, attach worksheets, and add quiz questions.

### Configure Pricing

Click **Pricing** → **Add Pricing Plan**. Create a "One-Time Payment" plan at $197. Add an "Early Bird" plan at $97 (limited to first 50 students).

### Build the Sales Page

Use this structure:
1. **Headline:** The transformation (not the topic). "Automate Your Business and Reclaim 20 Hours Per Week" beats "Master Make.com in 4 Weeks."
2. **Problem statement:** 3-4 sentences about the pain your student experiences
3. **Solution overview:** What the course delivers
4. **Module breakdown:** Each module with 2-3 bullet points
5. **Social proof:** Testimonials, results, or credentials
6. **Pricing:** Clear pricing with a guarantee
7. **FAQ:** 5-7 common objections addressed
8. **Call to action:** "Enroll Now" button

### CHECK-IN: Step 4 Complete

1. Course published on Teachable with all lessons
2. Pricing plan configured with early-bird discount
3. Sales page follows the structure above
4. Payment processing connected via Stripe

## Step 5: Launch and Market

### Pre-Launch (2 weeks before)

- Build an email waitlist using a free ConvertKit account
- Post teaser content on Twitter and LinkedIn daily
- Offer early-bird pricing for the first 50 buyers

### Launch Week

Send a 5-email sequence to your waitlist:
- **Day 1:** The Problem (why this matters)
- **Day 2:** The Solution (your approach)
- **Day 3:** The Course (what is inside)
- **Day 4:** Social Proof (results and testimonials)
- **Day 5:** Last Chance (early-bird closes)

### Post-Launch

Build an evergreen funnel:
- Free lead magnet (mini-course, cheat sheet, or template)
- 7-email nurture sequence
- Limited-time offer or bonus for new subscribers

### CHECK-IN: Step 5 Complete

1. Email waitlist with 50+ subscribers
2. 5-email launch sequence sent
3. At least 5 course sales within the first week

## Step 6: Scale with a Second Course and Subscription

### Identify Your Next Course Topic

Survey your existing students: "What would you learn next?" The most common answer is your next course topic.

### Launch a Subscription Tier

Add a $29-49/month subscription that includes: access to all courses, monthly live Q&A sessions, a private community, and new content updates.

### Build the Community

Use Circle.so (free tier) or Discord (free) to create a student community. Post weekly prompts, share student wins, and answer questions.

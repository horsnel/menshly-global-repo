## Step 2: Research and Write Your First Script

The script is the most important element of your video. Fliki handles production — your job is to provide a script worth producing. A mediocre script produced perfectly will underperform a great script produced adequately. Invest your time here.

### Topic Research Method

Open YouTube and search your niche keyword. Sort results by View Count. Look for videos with these characteristics:

- Over 100,000 views (proven demand for the topic)
- Uploaded within the last 12 months (topic is still relevant)
- Created by a channel with fewer than 200,000 subscribers (the topic is not dominated by mega-creators)

Write down the titles of the top 10 videos. Identify patterns — what topics appear multiple times? What angles are overused? What angles are missing? Your video should cover a proven topic from a unique angle.

### Script Template

Use this exact template for every script. It is optimized for YouTube retention — the structure keeps viewers watching through the entire video.

**Section 1 — Hook (first 5 seconds, approximately 25 words):** Open with a surprising statistic, provocative question, or bold claim. Do not introduce yourself. Do not say "in this video." Get straight to the value.

> Example: "If you invested just $5 a day starting at age 25, you would have $387,000 by retirement. Most people never do this. Here is exactly why."

**Section 2 — Context (30 seconds, approximately 75 words):** Explain why the hook matters. Give the viewer a reason to keep watching. Promise a specific outcome by the end of the video.

> Example: "The difference between people who build wealth and people who stay broke is not income — it is a handful of tiny financial habits that compound over decades. By the end of this video, you will know the 7 habits that separate the wealthy from the wishing, and exactly how to start each one this week."

**Section 3 — Body (7-12 minutes, approximately 1,000-1,800 words):** Present 5-7 key points. Each point follows this structure:
- State the point clearly
- Provide a specific example, case study, or data point
- Explain how to apply it (actionable advice)
- Brief transition to the next point

**Section 4 — CTA (30 seconds, approximately 75 words):** Ask for a subscribe and comment. Be specific about what viewers should comment — specific questions generate significantly more engagement than generic "leave a comment below" prompts.

### Generate the Script with ChatGPT

Use this prompt to generate your first draft, then edit it heavily:

```
You are a YouTube scriptwriter for a faceless channel in the [NICHE] niche. Write a [LENGTH]-minute video script on: "[TOPIC]"

Requirements:
- Open with a hook in the first 5 seconds — a surprising fact or provocative question
- Use a direct, conversational tone — speak to the viewer as "you"
- Include 5-7 key points, each with a specific number, example, or data point
- Add a brief transition between each point
- End with: "Which of these surprised you most? Tell me in the comments — I read every one. And if you want more content like this, hit subscribe."
- Total word count: approximately [WORDS] words (150 words per minute of video)
- Do NOT include any greeting, intro, or "hey guys"
- Do NOT include any sponsor mention or ad read
- Format as plain text with clear section breaks using "##" headers
```

Replace [NICHE], [LENGTH], [TOPIC], and [WORDS] with your specific values. For a 10-minute video, target approximately 1,500 words.

**Critical step:** Read the generated script aloud. Edit any sentence that sounds awkward when spoken. Remove filler words. Add specific numbers where the AI was vague. Cut 10-15% of the content — tighter scripts produce better videos.

**Interactive check-in:** Your script should have a clear hook in the first sentence, 5-7 distinct points with examples, and a specific CTA at the end. Read the first 30 seconds aloud. Does it make you want to keep listening? If not, rewrite the hook before moving on.

## Step 3: Produce Your Video in Fliki AI

This is where Fliki transforms your script into a finished video. The process takes 10-15 minutes for a 10-minute video.

### Create a New Video Project

From the Fliki dashboard, click **New File**. Select **Video** as the content type. Name your project with your video title for easy reference. Click **Create**.

### Input Your Script

You have two options for getting your script into Fliki:

**Option A — Paste your script directly (recommended):** Click the text area on the left side of the editor. Paste your entire script. Fliki will automatically split the text into scenes, with each sentence or paragraph becoming a separate scene. This is the fastest method and gives you the most control over scene breaks.

**Option B — Use a URL:** Click **From URL** and paste a blog post or article URL. Fliki will extract the text and create scenes automatically. This works well if you are converting blog content into video, but you lose control over scene breaks and pacing.

### Configure Voice and Audio Settings

After pasting your script, Fliki assigns your default voice to all scenes. Verify this by playing the first scene. If the voice sounds right, it will be consistent across all scenes.

**Adjust pacing:** Click the **three-dot menu** on each scene and adjust the **Pause After** setting. Add 0.5-second pauses between sections and 1-second pauses at major transitions. These micro-pauses prevent the narration from feeling rushed and give viewers time to absorb key points.

**Add background music:** Click the **Music** tab in the right sidebar. Browse by mood — for finance and business content, select "Corporate" or "Ambient." For history and documentary content, select "Cinematic" or "Dramatic." Set the music volume to 10-15% of the voiceover volume. The music should create atmosphere without competing with the narration.

### Customize Visuals for Each Scene

Fliki automatically selects visuals for each scene based on the text content. These defaults are good, but customizing key scenes significantly improves viewer retention.

**How to customize visuals:**

1. Click on a scene in the timeline
2. Click the **Visual** tab in the right sidebar
3. You will see Fliki's auto-selected visual. To change it:
   - **Search:** Type a keyword in the search bar to find specific stock footage or images
   - **Upload:** Click **Upload** to add your own images (useful for data visualizations, charts, or branded graphics)
   - **AI Generate:** Click **AI Image** to generate a custom image from a text prompt (Premium feature)
4. Set the **Visual Duration** to match the narration length — typically 8-15 seconds per visual
5. Add **Ken Burns effect** (subtle zoom or pan) to static images — this prevents visual stagnation

**Visual optimization rules:**
- Change the visual every 8-15 seconds to maintain viewer attention
- Use text overlays for key statistics, names, and definitions — click **Text** in the visual editor
- Avoid using the same visual style in consecutive scenes — alternate between footage, images, and text-on-screen
- For data-heavy sections, create simple charts in Canva and upload them as custom visuals

### Add Subtitles

Fliki generates subtitles automatically. Click the **Subtitle** tab in the right sidebar to customize:

1. **Position:** Set to **Bottom Center** (standard YouTube position)
2. **Style:** Bold white text, 24-28pt font size, with a semi-transparent dark background
3. **Animation:** Set to **Word by Word** — this highlights each word as it is spoken, which increases reading engagement by 20-30% compared to static subtitles
4. **Accuracy:** Play through each scene and verify the subtitles match the audio exactly. Fliki's AI is accurate but occasionally misrenders numbers, technical terms, and brand names. Correct any errors by clicking on the subtitle text and editing it directly.

### Preview and Render

Click the **Preview** button in the top-right corner. Watch the entire video. Check for:

- Voice quality and pacing (does it sound natural?)
- Visual alignment (do the visuals match the narration content?)
- Subtitle accuracy (are all words correct?)
- Overall flow (does the video maintain your attention throughout?)

When satisfied, click **Export**. Select **1080p (Full HD)** for YouTube long-form content. The rendering time depends on video length — typically 3-5 minutes for a 10-minute video. Download the MP4 file when rendering completes.

**Interactive check-in:** Play your rendered video from start to finish without stopping. Does the first 5 seconds hook you? Do the visuals change frequently enough to hold attention? Are the subtitles readable on a mobile screen (check on your phone)? If any element fails, return to the editor, fix the issue, and re-render before uploading.

## Step 5: Create Click-Winning Thumbnails in Canva

Your thumbnail is the single highest-impact element for click-through rate. YouTube's algorithm weighs CTR heavily — a video with a 10% CTR will outperform a better video with a 3% CTR every time.

### Thumbnail Design Rules

1. **Three words maximum** of text on the thumbnail — large, bold, high contrast
2. **One dominant image or icon** — avoid clutter and visual noise
3. **Use contrast colors** against YouTube's white/red interface — yellow, teal, and orange perform best
4. **Show emotion or surprise** — even illustrated faces with expressive features outperform neutral images
5. **Test two variations** — create two thumbnails and track CTR for each over 48 hours

### Canva Thumbnail Workflow

1. Open Canva and search for "YouTube Thumbnail" (1280 x 720 template)
2. Choose a template with a bold layout — solid color background with one large text element
3. Replace the background color with your brand color or a high-contrast solid (yellow #F9FF00, red #FF0004, or teal #00C9A7)
4. Add your 2-3 word text in the center using a bold sans-serif font (Impact, Anton, or Oswald)
5. Add one relevant image or icon — either from Canva's library or an image you generated
6. Add a subtle border or shape element for visual structure
7. Download as PNG

### A/B Test Your Thumbnail

After uploading your video, go to YouTube Studio → Content → click your video → Thumbnail. If YouTube's A/B Test feature is available on your channel, click **Test & Compare** and upload both thumbnail variations. YouTube will serve different thumbnails to different viewers and report which one wins after 48 hours.

If the feature is not available, publish with Thumbnail A, track CTR for 48 hours, swap to Thumbnail B, and compare. Keep the winner.

**Expected CTR:** Above 8% for new channels (YouTube gives new channels a CTR boost). Above 5% for established channels. Below 3% means your thumbnail needs a complete redesign — not a minor tweak.

## Step 7: Optimize for the YouTube Algorithm

Producing videos is necessary but not sufficient. YouTube's algorithm decides whether your content reaches viewers. These optimizations are not optional.

### Upload Timing

YouTube gives the strongest algorithmic boost in the first 24-48 hours after upload. Schedule your videos to go live when your target audience is most active on YouTube.

1. Open YouTube Studio → Analytics → Audience
2. Look at the "When your viewers are on YouTube" heatmap
3. Find the darkest-shaded time slot (most viewers online)
4. Schedule uploads for 2 hours before that peak time

For most US-targeted education channels, the optimal window is Tuesday-Thursday, 2-4 PM EST. But verify with your own data after 4-5 uploads.

### Hook Optimization (First 5 Seconds)

YouTube tracks "audience retention" — the percentage of viewers still watching at each second. The steepest drop-off happens in the first 5 seconds. If you lose 40%+ of viewers in 5 seconds, your hook is failing.

Check your retention curve in YouTube Studio → Analytics → Engagement → Audience Retention. If the first 5 seconds show a sharp drop, rewrite your hook using this formula:

1. Start with a surprising number or statistic
2. Immediately state why it matters to the viewer
3. Promise a specific outcome by the end of the video

A well-optimized hook retains at least 70% of viewers through the first 15 seconds.

### The Pattern Interrupt Technique

Every 30-45 seconds, something on screen must change — a new visual, a zoom effect, a text overlay, a sound effect, or a shift in narration pacing. This prevents viewer fatigue and keeps retention high. Fliki makes pattern interrupts easy because each scene is a separate visual. Ensure your scenes are short (8-15 seconds each) rather than long (30+ seconds), which creates natural visual variety.

## Step 9: Advanced Fliki AI Techniques

Once you are comfortable with the basic workflow, these techniques will improve your video quality and production efficiency.

### Custom Voice Cloning

Fliki supports voice cloning on paid plans. Upload a 2-3 minute audio sample of yourself speaking, and Fliki creates a custom voice model. This gives your channel a unique, consistent voice that no other channel can replicate — which is valuable for brand recognition and YouTube's "reused content" policy. A unique voice clone proves your content is original.

To set up voice cloning: go to Fliki → Voices → Clone Voice → Upload your audio sample → Wait 10-15 minutes for processing → Select your cloned voice as the default for your project.

### Batch Production

Produce a week's worth of videos in a single 4-5 hour session. Write 3-4 scripts on Sunday, produce all videos in Fliki on Monday, create thumbnails on Monday evening, and schedule uploads for the entire week. Batch production eliminates the daily context-switching overhead and allows you to maintain consistency even during busy weeks.

### Fliki API for Automation

Fliki offers an API on Premium plans. If you are producing 5+ videos per week across multiple channels, use the API to automate video creation directly from a spreadsheet or Make.com scenario. The API accepts a script and configuration, renders the video, and returns a download URL — enabling fully automated production pipelines.

### Repurposing Content Across Platforms

Every Fliki video can be repurposed for multiple platforms:

- **YouTube Shorts:** Trim to 45-60 seconds in Fliki, switch to 9:16 aspect ratio, re-render
- **TikTok:** Same process as Shorts, add trending audio from TikTok's library
- **Instagram Reels:** Same process, add branded text overlay
- **Blog post:** Use your script as the blog post body, embed the YouTube video
- **Newsletter:** Summarize the key points in a 200-word email, link to the video

One script produces content for 5 platforms. This maximizes the return on every minute of production time.
